Gobi Manchurian






Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized cauliflower, cut into bite-sized florets
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2 tablespoons oil for stir-frying
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili sauce (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped spring onions and fresh cilantro for garnish


Steps:

  1. Start by coating the cauliflower florets with a mixture of all-purpose flour, cornstarch, red chili powder, black pepper, and salt. Make sure each floret is evenly coated.

  2. Heat oil for deep frying. Once hot, add the coated cauliflower florets and fry them until they turn golden brown and crisp. Remove excess oil by draining the florets on paper towels and set them aside.

  3. In a separate pan, heat two tablespoons of oil. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Sauté until aromatic.

  4. Toss in the finely chopped onions and bell peppers. Sauté until they become tender and slightly caramelized.

  5. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, red chili sauce, vinegar, sugar, and salt to the sautéed vegetables. Stir well to create a flavorful sauce.

  6. Now, introduce the fried cauliflower florets into the pan. Gently toss them in the sauce until they are well-coated.

  7. Garnish your Gobi Manchurian with freshly chopped spring onions and cilantro. Serve it hot as a delicious and visually appealing dish that's sure to impress your taste buds.
