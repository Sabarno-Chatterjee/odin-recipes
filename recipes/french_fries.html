French Fries






Ingredients:

  • Potatoes (2-4, depending on the desired quantity)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Salt (to taste)


Steps:

  1. 1. Start by peeling the potatoes if desired, or you can leave the skin on for a more rustic texture.

  2. 2. Cut the potatoes into long, thin strips or your preferred fry shape. Make sure they are relatively uniform in size to ensure even cooking.

  3. 3. Rinse the cut potatoes in cold water to remove excess starch. This helps the fries become crispier when fried.

  4. 4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large, deep pan to around 350-375°F (175-190°C).

  5. 5. Carefully add a batch of potato strips to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry in batches if necessary.

  6. 6. Fry the potatoes until they turn golden brown and crispy, which usually takes 3-5 minutes per batch.

  7. 7. Using a slotted spoon or tongs, remove the fries from the hot oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.

  8. 8. While the fries are still hot, season them with salt to taste. You can also add other seasonings like pepper, paprika, or garlic powder if desired.

  9. 9. Serve the French fries hot and crispy with your favorite dipping sauces like ketchup, mayonnaise, or vinegar.
