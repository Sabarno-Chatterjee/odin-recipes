Chicken Thukpa






Ingredients:

  • 6 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/2 lb rice noodles (preferably round like spaghetti)
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 inch piece ginger, peeled
  • 1 TBSP serrano chili pepper, stem removed and chopped
  • 1/2 cup seeded chopped tomato
  • 1/4 cup chopped shallots
  • 2 TBSP chopped cilantro
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp crushed Szechuan pepper
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 TBSP fresh lime juice
  • 1 TBSP olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cooked chicken breast
  • 1 cup julienne carrot
  • 1 cup julienne red pepper
  • 1 cup julienne celery
  • 2 cups baby spinach, packed


Steps:

  1. Bring the chicken broth to a boil in a dutch oven or large pot. Add dry rice noodles and turn off the heat. Leave for 10 minutes until softened. Remove the noodles from the broth so they do not over-cook. Reserve the broth.

  2. Make the soup paste by combining garlic, ginger, serrano pepper, tomatoes, shallots, cilantro, cumin, turmeric, Szechuan pepper, garam masala, coriander, lime juice, and olive oil in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth.

  3. Heat a soup pot, add the soup paste, and fry for 30 seconds or so. Pour in the reserved chicken broth and mix well. Bring to a boil and reduce heat to low.

  4. Add shredded chicken, julienne carrots, red pepper, celery, and baby spinach. Simmer for five minutes or until the carrots are tender. Season with salt.

  5. To serve, divide the rice noodles between 6 bowls. Ladle the soup over the noodles and garnish with fresh cilantro.
