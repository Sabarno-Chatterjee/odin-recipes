Chilli Paneer






Ingredients:

  • 250 grams paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2 tablespoons oil for stir-frying
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili sauce (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped spring onions and fresh cilantro for garnish


Steps:

  1. Start by coating the paneer cubes with a mixture of all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and salt. Make sure each piece is evenly coated.

  2. Heat oil for deep frying. Once hot, add the paneer cubes and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove excess oil by placing the fried paneer on paper towels and set aside.

  3. In a separate pan, heat two tablespoons of oil. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté until aromatic.

  4. Toss in the sliced onions and bell peppers. Sauté them until they become tender and slightly caramelized.

  5. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, red chili sauce, vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper to the sautéed vegetables. Stir well to create a flavorful sauce.

  6. Now, introduce the fried paneer cubes into the pan. Gently toss them in the sauce until they are well-coated.

  7. Garnish your Chilli Paneer with freshly chopped spring onions and cilantro, and serve it hot. This delightful dish is not just visually appealing but a flavor-packed indulgence that offers a delectable blend of creamy paneer and zesty sauce.
